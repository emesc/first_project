A fractal tree



Requirements before something can be considered a fractal:

  • It exhibits self-similarity.
  • It has fine structure at small scales that cannot be described by Euclidean geometry (basic shapes).
  • It is generated recursively.

Here goes a simple fractal tree branching a bit longer to the right than to the left, with little flowers at the end. Angle of the branches is determined by the horizontal location of the mouse cursor on the canvas.

So try moving your mouse across the image.
