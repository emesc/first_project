Sankey diagram

a control flow diagram



Recently, the New York Times posted a news about a supercomputer in Tokyo Institute of Technology being submerged in liquids in order to keep the electricity bill down. Apparently, no harm is done on the machine. This is no new news. A lot of efforts like this have been done all over the world. One of it is using mineral oil to keep the computer's temperature down.

Energy consumption has always been a hot topic. So let me dive in.

I tried to rummage around the interwebs to see a good dataset. One site led to another and I was able to catch a good one. The thing is the data still has to be cleaned up for me to be able to use it with d3. Good news, OpenRefine is there to help.





With its wiki tutorial, I was able to get what I need.

One way that this can be visualised is through a Sankey diagram. Perhaps the most famous example of this is Charles Minard's Map of Napoleon's Russian Campaign of 1812 where the flow map is overlayed on a geographical map.





Here's my interactive result with nodes that can be dragged. You can also hover your mouse on the links to see which goes into where.
