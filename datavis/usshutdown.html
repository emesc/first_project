US Shutdown of 2013

Who to blame?



For 16 days, from 1st to 16th of October 2013, the United States federal government entered a shutdown for the first time since 1995. This happened after the Congress failed to pass Appropriations Bill for fiscal year 2014. If the Congress and the president did not reach an agreement and enact one or more legislation appropriating funds, a Continuing Resolution for the interim authorization of appropriations for fiscal year 2014 can be passed instead. None of this was passed until 17th of that month when an interim (temporary, provisional) appropriations bill was signed into law.


Sadly, only those government employees regarded 'excepted' under the Antideficiency Act were continued. About 1.3 million federal workers were asked to report even without known salary dates.


The Centre for the Study of Elections and Democracy at Brigham Young University in Utah was quick to conduct a survey between 2 October and 7 October of the same year, 2013. They invited members of the Utah Voter Poll panel, a group of voters who were recruited at polling places across Utah on Election Day in 2004, 2006, 2008, 2010, and 2012, to complete the online survey. One of the more interesting survey question goes like this:


On October 1, the national government shut down for the first time since 1995. In your opinion, who is the most responsible for the current shutdown? You may feel that multiple groups or individuals share responsibility. Please choose the group or individual you think is most responsible.
